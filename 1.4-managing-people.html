Staffing & Recruitment AO1

Internal recruitment: filling a vacancy from within. External recruitment: filling a vacancy from outside.

Internal recruitment
AdvantagesDrawbacks
Cheaper and faster than externalLimits the pool of new ideas/skills
Candidate familiar with cultureCreates another vacancy to fill
Motivates existing staffMay cause resentment among unsuccessful candidates
On-the-job training
AdvantagesDrawbacks
Cheaper, no external venues neededProductivity may be lost while learning
Directly relevant to the actual roleQuality depends on the trainer's skill

Organisational Design AO1

TermDefinition
Span of controlNumber of subordinates a manager is directly responsible for
Chain of commandLine of authority/communication top to bottom
DelegationPassing authority/responsibility down to a subordinate
CentralisationDecision-making concentrated at the top
DecentralisationDecision-making spread to lower levels
Wide span of control
AdvantagesDrawbacks
Fewer management layers, lower costsManager may struggle to supervise everyone
Encourages delegationLess individual attention per employee

Motivation Theory AO1

TheoristKey idea
TaylorWorkers motivated mainly by money
MayoWorkers motivated by social factors
MaslowHierarchy: physiological → safety → social → esteem → self-actualisation
HerzbergHygiene factors prevent dissatisfaction; motivators drive real motivation
Financial motivation methods
AdvantagesDrawbacks
Directly incentivises outputCan encourage quantity over quality
Easy to measure and link to resultsMay not sustain motivation long-term
Non-financial motivation methods
AdvantagesDrawbacks
Can create longer-lasting motivationHarder to measure direct impact
Lower direct cost than pay increasesTakes time to embed into culture
