This page exists because the command word tells you exactly what the examiner wants. Same content knowledge, wrong structure = lost marks. Learn this once, use it for every topic.

Side note: across every command word above Define, the main marks are NOT just for knowing the theory — they're for applying it to the specific business/scenario given in the question. Generic theory with no link to the case study will always be capped low. Always reference details from the scenario in your Example/Application step.
Define
2 marks
A precise definition of a term or phrase. Nothing else.
Define the term "price elasticity of demand." (2 marks)
A measure of the responsiveness of quantity demanded to a change in price.
Calculate
4 marks
A numeric answer from given data, with workings shown.
  1. State the formula
  2. Substitute the numbers
  3. Show the working line
  4. State the final answer with units/label
Calculate the break-even output. (4 marks)
Break-even = Fixed Costs ÷ Contribution per unit = $40,000 ÷ $10 = 4,000 units
Construct
4 marks
An accurately drawn, labelled diagram.
  • Correct diagram type for the question
  • Axes labelled correctly
  • Curves/lines correctly shaped and positioned
  • Key points labelled
Explain
4 marks
A brief explanation of a cause or effect, backed by a reason or example.
  1. Point
  2. Example/Scenario
  3. Explain
  4. Link
Explain one reason why a business might use penetration pricing. (4 marks)
One reason why a business might use penetration pricing is to gain market share quickly. For example, a newly launched smartphone brand entering a saturated market may set its price well below established competitors. As a result, price-sensitive customers are attracted away from competitors, helping the business build a loyal customer base. Furthermore, once this base is established, the business can gradually raise prices.
Analyse
6 marks
A short chain of reasoning — cause AND effect, in context. No evaluation/judgement yet.
  1. Point
  2. Example/Application
  3. Explain
  4. Link/Effect

Two of these chains is often a good way to hit 6 marks with depth, rather than four shallow points.

Discuss
8 marks
Reasoning in context, showing cause/effect plus a brief assessment of competing factors.
  1. Argument 1 (for) — point, example/scenario, explain
  2. Argument 2 (against) — point, example/scenario, explain
  3. Brief weighing — which matters more, given the context
Assess
10 / 12 marks
Balanced, wide-ranging reasoning with awareness of competing arguments, leading to a supported judgement.
  1. Argument for — point, example/scenario, explain, link
  2. Argument against — same structure
  3. Judgement — which side wins and why, referencing the context

The judgement must be justified, not just asserted.

Evaluate
20 marks
Fully developed reasoning with awareness of competing factors, leading to a perceptive conclusion.
  1. Brief intro
  2. Argument 1 — point → example/scenario → explain → effect
  3. Counter-argument to Arg 1
  4. Argument 2 — a second distinct point
  5. Counter-argument to Arg 2
  6. Judgement + Conclusion — sum up, state which side wins / what the business should do

Common mistake: four "for" points and one weak "against" at the end is NOT evaluation — it's analysis with a label.

Phrase Bank

Point

One reason... / One advantage...

Example

This can be seen... / This is because...

Explain

As a result → This leads to → Furthermore

Counter-Argument

However... / This depends on...

Evaluation (Judgement + Conclusion)

Even though option A does this [benefits], this is the issue [problems], which is why option B is better as it does this [benefits], even though [side effects]. Overall, the business should do this...
