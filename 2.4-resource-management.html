Production, Productivity & Efficiency AO1

To understand how effectively a business turns inputs (labour, materials, capital) into outputs, which directly affects cost per unit and competitiveness.

TermDefinition
ProductionThe process of turning inputs into outputs (goods/services)
ProductivityOutput per worker (or per input) over a period
EfficiencyProducing output at the lowest possible cost/waste of resources

Labour productivity = Total output ÷ Number of employees

Improving labour productivity
AdvantagesDrawbacks
Lowers unit costs, improving competitivenessMay require investment in training/technology, which is costly upfront
Can increase capacity without hiring more staffPushing too hard can reduce quality or harm staff morale

Capacity Utilisation AO2

To measure how much of a business's maximum possible output is actually being used, helping identify spare capacity or overstretching.

Capacity utilisation = (Actual output ÷ Maximum possible output) × 100

High capacity utilisation
AdvantagesDrawbacks
Fixed costs spread over more output, lowering unit costLittle spare capacity to respond to sudden demand increases
Signals efficient use of resources to investorsIncreased risk of machine/staff breakdown from overuse

Inventory (Stock) Control AO2

To manage stock levels so a business has enough to meet demand without tying up excessive cash in unsold inventory.

Just-in-time (JIT): stock/materials arrive only as needed for production, minimising stock held.

Just-in-time (JIT) stock control
AdvantagesDrawbacks
Reduces storage costs and cash tied up in stockHighly vulnerable to supply chain disruption
Reduces risk of holding obsolete/wasted stockRequires very reliable, often more expensive suppliers
Improves cash flow since less capital is tied upLittle buffer against sudden demand spikes

Quality Management AO1

To ensure products/services consistently meet customer expectations, protecting reputation and reducing costly errors/returns.

ApproachDescription
Total Quality Management (TQM)A whole-business culture where every employee is responsible for quality
KaizenContinuous, incremental improvement involving all staff
Quality controlChecking output at the end of production for defects
Total Quality Management (TQM)
AdvantagesDrawbacks
Reduces costly defects/returns by catching issues earlierRequires significant training and cultural change
Can improve brand reputation and customer loyaltyCan slow down processes initially as new checks are introduced
