Costs & Revenue AO1

To understand the building blocks needed to calculate profit and plan pricing/output decisions.

TermDefinition
Fixed costsCosts that don't change with output (e.g. rent)
Variable costsCosts that change directly with output (e.g. raw materials)
Total costsFixed costs + Variable costs
RevenuePrice × Quantity sold
Contribution per unitSelling price − Variable cost per unit

Break-Even Analysis AO2

To identify the level of output at which total revenue equals total costs, so a business knows the minimum it must sell to avoid a loss.

Break-even output = Fixed costs ÷ Contribution per unit

To the left of break-even = loss (costs exceed revenue). To the right = profit (revenue exceeds costs).

Using break-even analysis
AdvantagesDrawbacks
Shows the minimum sales needed to avoid a lossAssumes all output is sold — ignores unsold stock
Helps set sales targets and assess new venture viabilityAssumes costs/prices stay constant, which is rarely true
Useful for securing finance — shows lenders viabilityDoesn't account for non-linear cost changes (e.g. bulk discounts)

Cash Flow Forecasting AO2

To predict the timing of cash inflows and outflows, so a business can identify potential cash shortages before they happen — even a profitable business can fail if it runs out of cash.

TermDefinition
Cash inflowMoney coming into the business (e.g. sales receipts)
Cash outflowMoney leaving the business (e.g. paying suppliers, wages)
Net cash flowInflows − Outflows for a period
Closing balanceOpening balance + Net cash flow for the period
Using cash flow forecasts
AdvantagesDrawbacks
Identifies cash shortages in advance, allowing time to arrange financeBased on estimates/assumptions that can be wrong
Required by banks when applying for a loan/overdraftDoesn't show profitability, only cash timing

Budgets AO1

To set financial targets for income and expenditure, helping control spending and measure performance against a plan.

TypeDescription
Income budgetTarget for sales/revenue over a period
Expenditure budgetPlanned spending limit over a period
VarianceThe difference between budgeted and actual figures (favourable or adverse)

Key terms to know cold

Fixed costsCosts that don't change with output
Variable costsCosts that change directly with output
Contribution per unitSelling price minus variable cost per unit
Break-even outputThe output level where total revenue equals total costs
Net cash flowCash inflows minus cash outflows for a period
VarianceThe difference between budgeted and actual figures
