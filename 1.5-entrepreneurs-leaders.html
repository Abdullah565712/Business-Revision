The Role of an Entrepreneur AO1

To identify a business opportunity, take on the risk of starting a venture, and organise resources to exploit it.

Key functions: spotting opportunities, taking risk, organising resources, innovating.

Entrepreneur typically owns the business and takes financial risk. Leader/manager directs existing operations without necessarily owning the business.

Entrepreneurial Motives & Characteristics AO1

Motives: financial gain, independence, pursuing a passion, social/ethical motives.

Characteristics: risk-taking, innovative, resilient, determined, good communicator.

Being highly risk-tolerant
AdvantagesDrawbacks
Pursues ambitious, high-reward opportunitiesHigher chance of significant financial loss
Acts decisively where risk-averse competitors hesitateMay underestimate genuine warning signs

Business Choices AO2

Opportunity cost: the next best alternative given up when a choice is made. Every decision involves an opportunity cost.

Trade-offs: balancing competing objectives, e.g. growth vs profit, cost vs quality, short-term vs long-term.

Prioritising growth over short-term profit
AdvantagesDrawbacks
Builds market share and long-term positionReduces short-term profit/cash available
Can lead to economies of scale over timeGrowth funded by debt increases financial risk
Increases brand recognitionRapid growth can strain management capacity
