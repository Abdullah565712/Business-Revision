Economic Influences AO1

To understand how factors outside a business's control affect demand, costs, and decision-making.

FactorEffect on business
Economic growth/recessionAffects overall consumer demand and confidence
InflationRising costs and potential wage demands; reduces real purchasing power
Interest ratesAffects cost of borrowing and consumer willingness to spend vs save
Exchange ratesAffects cost of imports and competitiveness of exports
UnemploymentAffects consumer spending power and labour availability

This is the business cycle: economies move through boom, slowdown, recession, and recovery — businesses must adapt strategy at each stage.

Legislation AO1

To understand how laws shape what businesses can and must do, affecting costs and operating practices.

AreaExamples
Employment lawMinimum wage, working hours, discrimination protections
Consumer protectionProduct safety, accurate advertising, refund rights
Competition lawPreventing monopolies/anti-competitive practices
Environmental lawEmissions limits, waste disposal rules
Operating under stricter regulation
AdvantagesDrawbacks
Protects consumers/employees, building long-term trustIncreases compliance costs (legal, administrative)
Creates a fairer, more predictable competitive environmentCan slow down decision-making and innovation

The Competitive Environment AO2

To understand how the number and behaviour of competitors affects a business's pricing power and strategy.

  • Monopoly: one dominant firm controls the market, high pricing power.
  • Oligopoly: a few large firms dominate, often competing on non-price factors.
  • Perfect/high competition: many firms, little individual pricing power.
Operating in a highly competitive market
AdvantagesDrawbacks
Drives innovation and efficiency to stay competitiveLimits pricing power, squeezing profit margins
Keeps prices fair for consumersHigher risk of business failure for weaker competitors

Key terms to know cold

InflationA sustained rise in the general price level
Business cycleThe recurring pattern of boom, slowdown, recession and recovery
MonopolyA market dominated by one firm with high pricing power
OligopolyA market dominated by a few large firms
