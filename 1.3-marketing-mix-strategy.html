Marketing Objectives & Product Life Cycle AO1

To set clear goals for marketing activity and choose an overall approach to achieve them.

Product life cycle: Introduction → Growth → Maturity → Decline. Marketing strategy shifts at each stage.

Extension strategies: new packaging, new markets, price changes, product modification — used to prolong maturity.

Boston Matrix AO2

  • Star: high growth, high share
  • Cash Cow: low growth, high share
  • Problem Child: high growth, low share
  • Dog: low growth, low share
Using the Boston Matrix
AdvantagesDrawbacks
Helps balance a product portfolioMarket share/growth alone don't capture full picture
Simple visual tool for decisionsData can be hard to obtain accurately
Highlights which products need investmentRisk of withdrawing "Dogs" too early

Promotion and Branding AO1

Above the line: paid advertising via mass media. Below the line: sponsorship, sales promotions, direct marketing, PR.

Branding: to create a distinct identity that builds trust, loyalty, and allows premium pricing.

Strong branding
AdvantagesDrawbacks
Builds loyalty and repeat purchasesExpensive to build and maintain
Allows premium pricingA scandal can damage the whole brand
Differentiates from competitorsCan make repositioning harder later

Product/Service Design AO1

To ensure products meet customer needs and stand out from competitors.

Investing heavily in design
AdvantagesDrawbacks

Improves satisfaction and loyaltyRisk design doesn't match expectations
Can extend product life cycleCompetitors may copy design elements
