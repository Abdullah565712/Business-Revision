Mass vs Niche Markets AO1

Mass market: large-scale market aimed at a broad range of customers. High volume, lower prices, intense competition, economies of scale.

Niche market: a small, specialised segment of a market with specific needs. Lower volume, higher prices, less competition, easier loyalty.

Niche market strategy
AdvantagesDrawbacks
Less direct competitionSmaller customer base limits growth/revenue
Can charge premium pricesVulnerable if the niche shrinks/trends change
Easier to build loyalty & brand identityHarder to achieve economies of scale
Mass market strategy
AdvantagesDrawbacks
Economies of scale lower unit costsIntense competition, often price wars
Large potential customer base & revenueHarder to differentiate and build loyalty
Brand can become widely recognisedLower profit margins per unit

Market Size & Market Share AO1

Market size = total value or volume of sales within a market.

Market share = sales of a business as a % of total market sales.

Market share = (business sales ÷ total market sales) × 100

Dynamic Markets AO1

Dynamic markets are markets that change rapidly due to factors such as technology, consumer tastes, competition and regulation.

  • Online retailing: lower overheads, more price transparency, global reach.
  • Innovation: can expand a market or create entirely new ones.
Operating in a dynamic market
AdvantagesDrawbacks
First-movers capture market share quicklyConstant change increases risk and uncertainty
Opportunities for innovation, new revenue streamsRequires ongoing investment to keep up
Can adapt and find new niches as old ones declineSlower competitors may fail and exit suddenly

Risk vs Uncertainty AO1

Risk: a danger where the probability is known or can be estimated.

Uncertainty: a danger where the probability is unknown.

Competitive Advantage AO1

A factor that allows a business to outperform competitors, such as lower costs, stronger branding, superior quality or better customer service.

Pursuing competitive advantage
AdvantagesDrawbacks
Allows premium pricing, higher marginsCan be expensive to build
Builds loyalty, reduces price competition impactCompetitors may eventually copy/erode it
Harder for new entrants to competeOver-focusing on one advantage leaves gaps elsewhere

Purpose of Market Research AO1

To identify and anticipate customer needs and wants, quantify likely demand, and gain insight into consumer behaviour.

Primary vs Secondary Research AO1

Primary: surveys/questionnaires, focus groups, interviews, product trials.

Secondary: websites/social media, news/media, reports, databases.

Primary research
AdvantagesDrawbacks
Specific to the business's exact needsExpensive and time-consuming
Up-to-date dataRequires expertise to design well
Can be kept confidential from competitorsSmaller sample sizes than secondary sources
Secondary research
AdvantagesDrawbacks
Cheaper and faster to accessMay not be specific to the exact question
Often large-scale/national dataCould be outdated by time of use
Useful before spending on primary researchAvailable to competitors too

Sampling Methods AO2

To select a smaller group from a population that can give reliable, useful insight into the whole population without surveying everyone.

MethodStrengthWeakness
RandomLess biasMay not represent all groups
QuotaQuick and inexpensiveInterviewer bias possible
StratifiedMore representativeMore time-consuming

Product vs Market Orientation AO1

To describe whether a business starts with the product or starts with customer needs.

Product orientation: focuses on developing the product it believes is good, less focus on customer wants.

Market orientation: researches customer wants first, then designs the product to meet them.

Market Mapping AO2

To visually identify gaps in a market that a business could exploit, by comparing competitors on two key variables.

A visual tool plotting competitors against two key variables (e.g. price vs quality) to identify gaps in the market.

Market Segmentation AO1

To divide a market into groups of consumers with similar characteristics, so a business can target its product and marketing more effectively.

  • Demographics (age, income, gender)
  • Geography
  • Lifestyle/psychographics
  • Behaviour (usage patterns, brand loyalty)
Market segmentation
AdvantagesDrawbacks
More targeted, effective marketingCostly to research segments accurately
Helps identify gaps competitors are missingSegments may be too narrow, limiting sales
Improves customer satisfactionSegments need constant re-evaluation

Product Differentiation AO1

Making a product distinct from competitors, reducing direct price competition and building brand loyalty.

Product differentiation
AdvantagesDrawbacks
Reduces direct price competitionCan be costly (innovation, marketing, design)
Builds loyalty, reduces price elasticityRisk it doesn't appeal to enough customers
Justifies premium pricingCompetitors may imitate over time
